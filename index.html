Modeling an ECE Diagnostic for SPARC

Through the Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program (UROP), I designed an ECE diagnostic for SPARC at the Plasma Science & Fusion Center under the guidance of Prof. Anne White and Dr. Nathan Howard. I edited and wrote data-verified code in Python to assess analytic theory for predicting and modeling the optical system and its gaussian beam parameters. In doing so, I engineered solutions within physical and spatial restraints in collaboration with other researchers. (Image on the left courtesty of MIT News)



Characterizing the phase difference between the density and electrostatic potential in TJ-II

Through MIT International Science and Technology Initiatives (MISTI), I conducted research under the guidance of Dr. Arturo Alonso. Specifically, I characterized the phase difference between the density and electrostatic potential in the TJ-II stellarator with the heavy ion beam probe diagnostic to study how plasmas lose energy via turbulence. In doing so, I created and improved MATLAB codes to realize that the data is approaching the necessary quality to do this characterization. I also produced easily-readable graphs that inspired and informed future experimental campaigns. (Image on the left from TJ-II Wikipedia)



STEP Validation

In collaboration with Prof. White, Dr. Chris Holland, and Dr. Pablo Rodríguez Fernández, I have reviewed hundreds of scholarly papers about tokamaks to validate STEP against any published temperature profile. I am validating STEP against this data to determine how effective STEP is for designing future reactors.



Degradation of Plasma Facing Mirrors in ASDEX Upgrade

Under the supervision of Prof. White, I conducted research on the degradation of plasma facing mirrors used in ECE by designing and running experiments on a replica of ASDEX’s optical system to model gaussian beam parameters to verify the model with collected and analyzed data. (Image on the right courtesy of Max Planck Institute for Plasma Physics)



Thomson-ECE Discrepancy

Under the supervision of Prof. White, I reviewed academic papers about ECE diagnostics to conduct research on the Thomson-ECE Discrepancy to learn how to interpret data from ECE diagnostics. (Image on the right from DOI:10.1063/1.1316748)
